## 📝 Task Details

### 🎨 Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic

In Task 1, I designed a digital literacy infographic to visually explain the concept and importance of digital literacy for students. The infographic was created using Canva and focused on presenting information in a clear, engaging, and easy-to-understand format. It included key sections such as the definition of digital literacy, examples of useful digital tools, and essential safe internet practices.

The first section explained digital literacy as the ability to use digital technologies effectively, responsibly, and safely in everyday life. The second section highlighted commonly used tools like online document editors, coding platforms, and learning resources that help students improve productivity and academic performance. The third section focused on internet safety, including tips such as using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, and protecting personal information online.

While designing the infographic, I focused on maintaining a clean layout, proper color balance, and the use of icons to enhance visual appeal. One challenge I faced was organizing the content in a limited space without making it look cluttered. This task helped me understand how visual communication can make complex information easier to understand and more impactful for the audience..

### 📧 Task 4 – Email Etiquette & Communication

In Task 4, I focused on understanding professional communication through email writing and responsible behavior on social media. I drafted two formal emails: one requesting an extension for an assignment submission and another expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity. Both emails were structured properly with a clear subject line, polite greeting, well-organized body, and a professional closing. This helped me understand the importance of clarity, tone, and respect while communicating in a formal environment.

In addition to email writing, I created a checklist of do’s and don’ts for responsible social media usage. The checklist included important practices such as maintaining a professional profile, avoiding sharing personal information, and being respectful in online interactions. It also highlighted common mistakes like spreading misinformation or engaging in inappropriate behavior online.

A situation where poor digital communication can cause problems is when a student sends an informal or unclear email to a professor. For example, using casual language, missing subject lines, or being disrespectful can lead to misunderstandings or rejection of requests. This task helped me realize that proper communication plays a key role in building a positive impression and avoiding unnecessary issues in academic and professional settings.

### 🔐 Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness

In Task 5, I explored the concept of cybercrime with a focus on phishing, which is one of the most common online threats. Phishing involves attackers pretending to be trusted organizations to trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. Through this task, I understood how such attacks are carried out and how easily users can become victims if they are not careful.

While researching this topic, I was surprised to learn how realistic phishing messages can appear, making it difficult to distinguish them from genuine communication. Many users fall victim due to urgency created in such messages, such as account suspension warnings or fake offers. This highlights the importance of awareness and careful verification before taking any action online.

One habit I will change after completing this task is being more cautious while clicking on links or responding to unknown messages. I will always verify the source before sharing any personal or financial information. This task helped me understand the importance of cybersecurity and encouraged me to adopt safer digital practices in my daily life.
